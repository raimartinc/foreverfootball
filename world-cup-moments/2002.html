World Cup 2002: Brazil’s Asian Glory

Korea/Japan ’02 was Brazil’s fifth crown—eight wild tales of comebacks, cheeky dives, clutch saves, and pure payback that still get us buzzing.

1. Ronaldo’s Redemption Final

June 30, 2002—Yokohama, 69,029 rammed in tight—Brazil vs. Germany, final showdown. Ronaldo, 25, had ’98’s horror show—fit, collapse, nothing—now he’s back with a vengeance. Sixty-seventh minute—Rivaldo fires, Oliver Kahn spills it—Ronaldo’s on it, slots it home—1-0. Twelve minutes later—Kleberston threads him through—he bangs another—2-0. Germany push—Oliver Neuville, Jens Jeremies buzzing—but Marcos shuts the door—Brazil’s fifth star lands, Ronaldo’s eight goals nab the Golden Boot.

Yokohama’s losing it—yellow everywhere—Ronaldo’s grinning—“I’m back, mates”; Luiz Felipe Scolari’s chuffed—“He’s our heart.” Clips roll—Kahn’s fumble, Ronaldo’s cool finish—’98’s ghost is toast. “He killed us,” Michael Ballack groaned; Roberto Carlos is like—“He’s the man.” Four years of knee hell—Ronaldo rises like a phoenix. “He’s a legend,” Pelé’s shouting—those replays, buzz cut gleaming, still hit you hard—Brazil rule Asia, mate.

2. South Korea’s Dream Run

June 18, 2002—Daejeon, 38,289 going wild—South Korea vs. Italy, round of 16. Christian Vieri nods—18th—1-0 Italy—looks done, yeah? Eighty-eighth minute—Seol Ki-hyeon scrambles one—1-1—extra time—119th, Ahn Jung-hwan leaps—heads past Gianluigi Buffon—2-1—Italy’s out. Quarters—June 22, Gwangju—Spain—0-0—pens, 5-3—Joaquín fluffs it—semis, lose 1-0 Germany. Fourth place—first Asian semi ever.

Red’s taking over—Korea’s flipping mental—Ahn’s grinning—“For the fans”; Guus Hiddink’s buzzing—“They’re warriors.” Clips hit—Ahn’s header, Buffon grasping—Italy’s stunned. “They robbed us,” Francesco Totti fumed—red card didn’t help; Park Ji-sung’s like—“We believed.” Turkey nab third—3-2—but Korea’s run—nuts. “They’re legends,” Pelé said—still gets you in the guts, mate—Asia’s pride flies high.

3. Senegal’s France Shock

May 31, 2002—Seoul, 62,561 buzzing—France vs. Senegal, the opener. France—’98 champs, Euro 2000—strutting in, but Senegal’s got Papa Bouba Diop lurking. Thirty-first minute—he nicks it off Emmanuel Petit, slots past Fabien Barthez—1-0. France push—Zidane’s half-fit, Thierry Henry, David Trezeguet firing blanks—Senegal dig in—biggest upset ever, champs crash out in groups.

Seoul’s rocking—Senegal’s green’s bouncing—Diop’s dancing shirtless—“For Africa”; Bruno Metsu’s grinning—“We stunned ‘em.” Clips roll—Diop’s tap-in, Barthez flapping—France are rattled. “They outran us,” Henry said; El Hadji Diouf’s like—“We’re here.” Quarters—lose 1-0 Turkey—but that opener? Pure gold. “They’re magic,” Maradona reckoned—still bangs, mate—France humbled day one.

4. Ronaldinho’s England Lob

June 21, 2002—Shizuoka, 47,436 holding their breath—Brazil vs. England, quarter-final. Trevor Sinclair bangs—23rd—1-0 England—then Rivaldo levels—45th—1-1—half-time. Fifty-first minute—Ronaldinho, 22, picks it up—40 yards out—free-kick—lobs it—David Seaman’s caught flat—2-1—then—nuts—he’s off—second yellow—mistaken ID—Brazil hold—semis bound.

Shizuoka’s wild—yellow’s roaring—Ronaldinho’s grinning—“Just tried it”; Scolari’s chuckling—“He’s a wizard.” Clips hit—that lob, Seaman scrambling—England’s gutted. “He fooled me,” Seaman said—tears later; Ronaldo’s like—“Pure class.” Red card stings—still five goals total—semis, 1-0 Turkey—then final. “He’s unreal,” Beckham reckoned—still gets you laughing, mate—England out, Brazil roll.

5. Rivaldo’s Turkey Dive

June 3, 2002—Ulsan, 33,842 buzzing—Brazil vs. Turkey, group stage. Ronaldo nods early—50th—1-0—half-time—then Hasan Şaş levels—1-1—86th minute—Luizão’s fouled—Rivaldo’s up—slots the free-kick—2-1—then—holy hell—Hakan Ünsal boots it at his legs—he grabs his face, flops—Ünsal’s off—red—Brazil nick it—semis—1-0—Rivaldo again.

Ulsan’s mental—yellow’s buzzing—Rivaldo’s smirking—“For the win”; Scolari’s laughing—“He’s crafty.” Clips roll—strike, then that dive—Turkey’s fuming. “He cheated,” Ümit Davala spat; Ronaldo’s like—“We fought.” Five goals total—Rivaldo’s clutch—semis too—Turkey out early. “He’s a genius,” Pelé said—still cracks you up, mate—Brazil sneak through, cheek and all.

6. Beckham’s Argentina Revenge

June 7, 2002—Sapporo, 35,927 buzzing—England vs. Argentina, group stage. ’98’s ghost—Beckham’s red—haunts him—43rd minute—Michael Owen’s fouled—Becks steps up—pen—blasts past Pablo Cavallero—1-0. Argentina push—Juan Verón, Javier Zanetti probing—David Seaman holds firm—England top group—sweet payback.

Sapporo’s mental—white’s roaring—Beckham’s fist’s up—“For ’98”; Sven-Göran Eriksson’s chuffed—“He’s our man.” Clips roll—Becks’ rocket, Cavallero diving—pure redemption. “He nailed it,” Owen said; Gabriel Batistuta’s gutted—“Tough.” Quarters—lose 2-1 Brazil—but this? Massive. “He’s clutch,” Gary Lineker said—still gets you, mate—Argentina out, Becks smiles.

7. Brazil’s Turkey Thriller

June 3, 2002—Ulsan, 33,842 buzzing—Brazil vs. Turkey, group stage—same game as Rivaldo’s dive, but let’s rewind. Ronaldo nods—50th—1-0—half-time—then Hasan Şaş bangs one—1-1—game’s alive—86th minute—Luizão’s fouled—Rivaldo steps up—2-1—Turkey push—Alpay Özalan, Ümit Davala—Cafu holds—Brazil nick it—semis—1-0—Rivaldo again.

Ulsan’s wild—yellow’s roaring—Rivaldo’s smirking—“For the win”; Scolari’s chuffed—“We scrapped.” Clips hit—Ronaldo’s nod, Şaş’s rocket—then the dive chaos—Turkey’s gutted. “They edged us,” Rustu Recber said; Ronaldo’s like—“Team effort.” Five goals total—Rivaldo’s flair—semis too—Turkey bow early. “They’re tough,” Hiddink reckoned—still gets you, mate—Brazil’s grit shines.

8. Germany’s Kahn Wall

Rewind the tape—2002’s run, Germany’s all about Oliver Kahn, the big lad in goal. Group stage—June 1, Kobe—8-0 vs. Saudi Arabia—he’s a bloody fortress. Quarters—June 15, Kashima—1-0 over USA, saves galore—semis, June 25, Seoul—1-0 vs. South Korea, Michael Ballack’s goal, Kahn’s clean sheet. Final—June 30—he’s golden ‘til 67th—Rivaldo’s shot slips—Ronaldo pounces—2-0 Brazil. Eight goals conceded—Kahn’s Golden Ball’s still his.

Germany’s roaring—Kahn’s glaring—“We fought”; Rudi Völler’s nodding—“He’s our rock.” Clips roll—saves vs. USA, Korea—then that slip. “He’s unreal,” Landon Donovan said; Ballack’s gutted—“One mistake.” Seven clean sheets—’94 Yashin vibes—Kahn’s a beast. “He’s the best,” Ronaldo reckoned—those replays, diving everywhere, still get you—Germany fall, but Kahn’s a titan, mate.
